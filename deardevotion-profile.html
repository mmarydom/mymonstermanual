Locals refer it as the the Deer of Devotion, as it has body parts of a deer. They see this creature not as a monster, but as a divine figure. The village offer the creature a yearly sacrifice, in exchange for prosperous crops protected from both droughts and predators. It is unknown if it actually has some sort of power, but the village is known to have fruitful farms all year round for centuries.
